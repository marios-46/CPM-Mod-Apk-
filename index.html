Tuning fundamentals — a disciplined workflow

Tuning with unlimited parts can be intoxicating; resist the urge to change everything at once. A scientific workflow keeps your results meaningful and sharable:

  1. Establish a baseline: Run five consistent laps on your chosen track with stock settings. Log average lap time, top speed, and braking zone behavior.
  2. Change one variable: Adjust a single parameter (spring rates, camber, gear ratio) and re-run the same laps.
  3. Capture data: Use replays and split times. Note how lap time changed and whether handling improved or regressed.
  4. Iterate: Keep beneficial changes and revert harmful ones. Build a library of setups so you can share them with accuracy.

Discipline-specific tuning guidance

Drift builds: Aim for controlled oversteer. Soften rear dampers, increase steering lock, and use progressive throttle maps to avoid snap oversteer. Consider anti-lag or throttle mapping that smooths low-end torque.

Drag builds: Launch control and traction are king. Use short gears, stiff suspension for weight transfer, and wide rear tires. A good clutch mapping and RPM limiter tune can save precious tenths.

Circuit builds: Balance aero with corner speed. Add downforce for technical tracks and lower it for long straights. Adjust brake bias and differential settings for stable corner entry and exit.

Advanced tuning tools — what to track

  • Engine torque curve: shifting points and turbo maps.
  • Suspension travel and damping: how the car reacts mid-corner.
  • Tire temperatures: measure compound effectiveness and adjust pressures.
  • Weight distribution: moving ballast or removing items changes balance.

Troubleshooting & common problems

Mods are software — sometimes they break. Frequently reported issues and fixes:

  • Crashes on startup: Clear app cache, reinstall, or revert to a known working mod build.
  • Save corruption: Restore from backup and avoid mixing save files between different mod versions.
  • Multiplayer desync: Restrict to mod-friendly servers or host a private session.
  • Performance drops: Lower render distance, disable post-processing effects, and ensure no background apps compete for resources.
